Why E3 — rationale
Why not revenue or bookings?
Revenue is a lagging indicator. Bookings only measure Sharon's success. E3 measures whether the entire promise — book it, plan it, execute it — is delivered inside Momentus. It's the only metric that captures all three products working together.
What does E3 tell us that ARR doesn't?
ARR tells you what customers paid. E3 tells you whether they got what they paid for. An account that pays for all three products but executes operations on paper hasn't realized the platform's value — and is at high churn risk.
How does E3 align PM, Engineering, and Design?
Any feature that doesn't move E3 is a distraction. Any friction that breaks the BCI → FDI → EON chain is a product failure. E3 gives every team a shared definition of what "done" means for a venue.

Why BCI — grounded in the research
The core failure BCI addresses
The vertical research found that many venues use Momentus to track bookings but complete the actual contracting in email, Word, or separate CRMs. BCI only counts when the full intake-to-contract workflow completes inside the platform — it cannot be gamed by logging a booking that was really done elsewhere.
Why not total bookings created?
Total bookings is gameable: a sales rep can log a booking after the fact, or log it inside Momentus while doing the real work in Salesforce. BCI requires the process — lead qualification, availability check, proposal, contract — to happen inside Momentus. That's the signal that the platform replaced the external tool.
How BCI starts the E3 chain
BCI is the handoff trigger. When Sharon closes a booking in-platform, Emilie has something to pick up inside Momentus. If Sharon logs the booking after closing it in a CRM, the handoff is broken — and E3 cannot start cleanly. BCI measures whether Sharon gave Emilie a clean baton.

Why FDI — grounded in the research
The core failure FDI addresses
The vertical research explicitly states: "Elite customers tend to detail and share event needs in Word/Excel. Downstream this makes ops more manual." FDI only counts when Emilie documents and distributes the full plan inside Momentus — triggering a clean handoff to Owen.
Why not events created or functions added?
Creating a function record doesn't mean the plan is complete or shared. FDI requires both completion (all detail fields populated) and distribution (shared to ops inside the platform). A half-finished BEO sent via email is a failure state for E3.
How FDI connects to E3
FDI is the middle link. If Emilie's detail lives in Word, Owen has nothing to execute from inside Momentus, and E3 cannot complete. Every FDI event is an event Owen can pick up in-platform — the bridge between sales and operations.

An event counts only when task execution does not fall back to paper, radio, or email.

Why EON — grounded in the research
The core failure EON addresses
The vertical research is explicit: "AMER customers use more paper-based manual process for job assignment and communication." Owen's execution currently happens on printed sheets, radio, and email follow-up. EON rises only when that execution moves inside the platform — it cannot be gamed by creating tasks and never updating them.
Why EON is the hardest and most valuable metric
The value realization gap is largest in operations. Enterprise customers range "from using customized reports to MWO to no ops in Momentus due to usability, cost, and learning curve." EON directly measures whether that gap is closing — and it's the gap the convention center research identified as most underserved.
How EON completes E3
EON is the final link. BCI hands off a contracted booking. FDI hands off a detailed plan. EON completes the loop by executing that plan inside the platform. An event that passes BCI → FDI → EON is an E3 event. Owen is the last mile.

Why ORA — grounded in the research
The core failure ORA addresses
"Tell me the one thing your team does that takes the most clicks. That's where we start." — Andrew Blanda, APAC EAB. The director currently receives team performance through verbal briefings, email chains, and exported spreadsheets. ORA rises only when Momentus surfaces a dashboard she acts on — not one she ignores or bypasses with a manual pull.
Why not reports generated or sessions logged?
Generated ≠ actioned. A director can have reports auto-generated and never open them. Sessions measure presence, not value. ORA requires a subsequent decision or team direction traceable to platform data — that's the signal the director is using Momentus as an actual management tool, not an audit trail.
How ORA amplifies E3
The director is the institutional pressure that drives BCI, FDI, and EON adoption. When she can see Sharon's pipeline, Emilie's BEO completion rate, and Owen's task closure rate inside Momentus, she creates the organizational accountability that makes E3 move. ORA is the metric of her institutional leverage over the operational chain.

Why VIS — grounded in the research
The core failure VIS addresses
"It's all in my brain, and it'll walk out the door when I retire." The executive currently carries institutional knowledge that lives nowhere inside the platform. VIS rises when Momentus can show the GM or CFO their venue's full performance story — before that knowledge walks out the door, and before the renewal conversation defaults to a slide deck prepared by someone else.
Why not executive logins or general platform usage?
Generic sessions measure presence, not strategic value. VIS requires the executive to complete a strategic-level review — accessing revenue performance, E3 rate, utilization trends, or ROI data inside the platform. That's the signal Momentus is serving them directly, not just being reported on by an intermediary.
How VIS is the ultimate downstream of E3
Every booking Sharon closes, every function Emilie details, every task Owen completes flows into the venue intelligence the executive reviews. VIS is the proof that E3 created strategic value — not just operational efficiency. If the executive can't see the full picture inside Momentus, the renewal conversation happens on someone else's terms.

Grammar Principles Applied — Rosenberg Semantic IxD + OPD
Consistent Object Type. All three operational primary objects are Product type — the irreducible unit each product creates. Not People, not Money, not Institution. Per OPD Checkpoint 2, money is a lagging indicator; the object must be the leading signal closest to the human motivation.
Consistent Core Action. Create is the primary action across all three operational products. This is the E3 structural insight: the North Star only completes when all three creation events happen inside Momentus. If Sharon logs elsewhere, Emilie details in Word, or Owen prints work orders — E3 fails at that link.
Attribute Taxonomy follows OPD. Fixed / Dynamic / Structural mirrors the three-layer persona attribute structure grounded in Guarino's ontological commitment (fixed conditions), computational ontology (dynamic states), and Barry Smith's categories of being (structural status). Applied here to objects, not personas.
Strategic layer grammar shift. At the strategic layer, the primary object type shifts from Product to Information, and the primary action shifts from Create to Analyze (Director) or Evaluate (Executive). The strategic personas don't create operational artifacts — they consume and act on what the operational chain produces. This is the semantic boundary in the Rosenberg model between operational grammar and strategic grammar.
